Lions and Wolves: Team Hunters

Lions

Lions live in social prides on African grasslands and open woodland. They are apex land predators. Lionesses often work together to hunt animals such as zebra and wildebeest, while males help protect the pride. By keeping herbivore numbers in check, lions help prevent too much grazing in one area.

Wolves

Wolves live in family packs in forests, tundra, mountains, and grasslands. They eat deer, elk, rabbits, and smaller animals. Wolves are largely depleted in many parts of their old range and depend on active conservation. Their hunting makes herds move, which gives plants a chance to grow back.

Why predators matter

  1. They control herbivore populations.
  2. They help prevent overgrazing.
  3. They remove weak or sick prey.
  4. They create food for scavengers.
  5. They keep food webs balanced.
