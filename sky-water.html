Eagles and Frogs: Air and Water

Eagles: top predators of the air

Eagles live near forests, mountains, rivers, coasts, and open country. Their razor-sharp vision helps them see prey from high above. Their massive wingspans, strong feet, and hooked beaks help them hunt fish, small mammals, and birds. They build large nests high in trees or on cliffs.

Bald eagles have an amazing recovery story. DDT made their eggshells thin, but legal protection and the ban on DDT helped them return. Today, lead poisoning, habitat loss, and endangered-species policy changes can still make recovery harder.

Frogs: small animals, big warning signs

Frogs have a unique life cycle. Eggs hatch into water-dwelling tadpoles. Then tadpoles grow legs and become land-dwelling adults. Their powerful back legs help them jump. Frogs eat insects, while birds, fish, snakes, and mammals eat frogs.

Frogs have permeable skin that absorbs water and chemicals. This makes them highly sensitive to pollution, so they are warning signs for ecosystem health. The desert rain frog and other species face pressure from the pet trade and mining. Protecting wetlands helps frogs, clean water, and people.
