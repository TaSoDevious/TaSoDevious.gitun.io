Elephants: Gentle Giants

Elephants are the largest land animals. They have massive bodies, strong trunks, and tusks. Their trunks help them breathe, smell, drink, pick up food, and communicate. Tusks help them dig, move objects, and strip bark.

They live in close family herds led by experienced females. Elephants are very intelligent. They remember water sources, learn travel routes, communicate with deep sounds, and care for young elephants. They eat grasses, leaves, fruit, roots, and bark.

Elephants live in African savannas and forests, plus Asian forests and grasslands. As they walk and eat, they spread seeds, open paths, and create water holes. Many other animals benefit from this. Poaching and habitat loss are still serious problems.
